Projects

Below is a selection of my academic and competition projects in statistics, data analysis, and modeling.

  • Project 1 – IMDB Ratings Analysis

    Analyzed IMDB movie ratings to explore how genre, popularity, and runtime relate to user ratings, using R and multiple regression on scraped data.

  • Project 2 – MLB Lineup Construction

    Studied how hitter statistics vary by lineup position using RetroSheet and Baseball Reference data, and how real lineups align with classical baseball strategy.

  • Project 3 – DataFest 2023 (ABA Legal Help)

    Team-based project exploring an online legal advice platform dataset to uncover seasonal patterns in questions and inform resource allocation.

  • Project 4 – Simulation Study: Parametric vs Nonparametric

    Simulation study comparing Welch’s t-test and the Mann–Whitney–Wilcoxon test under varying distributional shapes, tail behavior, and effect sizes.

  • Project 5 – DataFest 2024 (CourseKata)

    Investigated missing data patterns in CourseKata learning platform data, showing that missingness was non-random and has implications for analysis.

  • Project 6 – FEC Election Funding Analysis

    Used FEC campaign finance data to examine whether better-funded candidates tend to win, including extensive data cleaning and logistic modeling attempts.

  • Project 7 – Principal Component Decomposition of Images

    Applied principal component decomposition to image data to study how efficiently different images can be represented with a limited number of components.
