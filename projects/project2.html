Project Overview

This project analyzes Major League Baseball (MLB) lineup construction and how hitter statistics vary by lineup position. Working with a partner, I hoped to build a model for optimal lineup construction, but the complexity of the problem and our level of statistical training led us to focus on describing and confirming existing patterns instead.

We used R throughout, drawing on data from RetroSheet and Baseball Reference, connected via the BaseballR package. A large portion of the work involved merging and aligning the two datasets, which required careful reformatting and explicit handling of inconsistencies in player names and identifiers.

Ultimately, we confirmed several classic ideas about lineup construction: hitters in the 1 and 2 spots tended to have the highest on‑base percentages, the 3‑hole hitter was often the best overall, and the 4 and 5 spots housed the strongest power hitters. More than anything, the project reinforced the importance of data cleaning, using multiple data sources, and supplementing analysis with background research.
