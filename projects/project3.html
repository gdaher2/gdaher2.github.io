Project Overview

This project was completed for the 2023 ASA DataFest, a weekend‑long data hackathon where undergraduate teams explore a large, complex dataset with minimal guidance. My team and I competed at RIT as members of the Society of Undergraduate Statistics Students at the University of Rochester.

The data came from the American Bar Association’s online legal advice platform and consisted of multiple related tables that were not pre‑cleaned for analysis. A substantial portion of our effort went into merging and reshaping the data so that different analytical questions could be answered effectively.

We ultimately focused on seasonality in the volume of questions and responses across the year. Our findings suggested that the platform could better allocate resources by aligning staffing and support with periods of higher demand. Personally, this project was also an opportunity to contribute as the only underclassman on the team, build confidence working under tight time constraints, and experiment with incorporating external data such as Google search trends.
