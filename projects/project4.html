Project Overview

This project is a simulation study comparing the performance of parametric and nonparametric methods in different data‑generating scenarios. In particular, I studied Welch’s t‑test versus the Mann–Whitney–Wilcoxon rank sum test under varying distributional shapes, tail behavior, and effect sizes.

The simulations were written in R and examined power and type I error across a range of sample sizes, asymmetries, and kurtoses. By systematically varying these conditions, I could see when the parametric assumptions held reasonably well and when nonparametric methods provided a more robust alternative.

This was my first full simulation study, and it required careful design of the data‑generating mechanisms as well as efficient code for running and summarizing thousands of iterations. It deepened both my understanding of the underlying theory and my comfort translating that theory into code.
