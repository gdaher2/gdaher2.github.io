Project Overview

In this project, I examined whether the better‑funded candidate in an election typically wins, using campaign finance data from the Federal Election Commission (FEC). I completed the work for an advanced statistical programming class and chose the topic out of personal interest in elections and political data.

A significant portion of the project involved cleaning and restructuring both the finance data and the election results, which were formatted more for human consumption than for analysis. Matching race results, handling paired outcomes, and ensuring that data were correctly aligned required extensive preprocessing.

I attempted to fit logistic regression models to election outcomes using funding information and related predictors. Although some models converged and appeared significant, they ultimately failed goodness‑of‑fit checks, underscoring the challenges of modeling complex election processes. The project was still valuable for the experience of working through practical obstacles, consulting the literature, and iterating on model design.
